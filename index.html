Artifact Summaries

1. Network Traffic Analysis Using Wireshark

Course: Network Essentials (IT-7003) | Term: Spring 2, 2023-2024 | Instructor: Thomas Determan

This hands-on technical assignment explores the Wireshark network analysis tool to capture and dissect live network packets. The paper covers foundational networking concepts including IPv4 vs. IPv6 addressing, MAC addresses, the OSI model, DNS operations, load balancing, and the role of DNS Security Extensions (DNSSEC) in protecting network integrity. A real-world packet capture from www.ford.com is analyzed to illustrate how data travels across the internet. This artifact demonstrates technical proficiency in network infrastructure analysis, a critical competency in IT-driven business environments.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: IT LiteracyTechnical AnalysisInformation Systems Understanding

2. Balanced Scorecard Case Analysis for Ragland Department Store

Course: Management Accounting (BUS-7800) | Term: Summer 1, 2023-2024 | Instructor: Professor Stephen Weiss

This collaborative research paper develops a Balanced Scorecard (BSC) framework for a fictional mid-sized retailer, Ragland Department Store, facing competitive pressure from national chains. The team evaluated four BSC perspectives — Financial, Customer, Internal Process, and Learning & Growth — and proposed performance measures aligned with Ragland's strategic goals. The paper also makes the case for BSC adoption to skeptical stakeholders and outlines implementation next steps. This artifact demonstrates competency in strategic performance management, financial analysis, and translating organizational vision into measurable objectives.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: Strategic ManagementFinancial AcumenPerformance Measurement

3. Honda Motor Co. Financial Performance Analysis (2015–2024)

Course: Data Analytics (IT-8000) | Term: Summer 2, 2023-2024 | Instructor: Professor Nicolas Bowersox

This final project delivers a comprehensive analysis of Honda Motor Co.'s financial performance over a decade (2015–2024), examining key metrics including Revenue, Cost of Goods Sold, Gross Profit, Operating Income, Net Income, and Earnings Per Share. The analysis identifies long-term trends, strategic strengths and weaknesses, and evaluates Honda's adaptability through varying economic cycles. Using data analytics techniques, the project translates raw financial data into actionable business insights. This artifact demonstrates proficiency in quantitative analysis, data visualization, and evidence-based strategic evaluation.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: Data AnalyticsFinancial Performance AnalysisStrategic Insight

Data Analytics — Final Project Report

4. Implementing Dynamic Pricing for Healthcare Billing Software

Course: Managerial Economics (BUS-7500) | Term: Fall 1, 2024-2025 | Instructor: Professor Joel Light

This final project proposes a usage-based dynamic pricing model for healthcare billing software, replacing a flat-rate structure that disadvantages both small clinics and large hospital systems. The model prices services based on patient volume, billing code complexity, and usage frequency. Drawing on economic theory — including demand elasticity and value-based pricing — the paper analyzes the limitations of the current model, presents a dynamic pricing solution, and forecasts its revenue and accessibility impact. This artifact demonstrates the ability to apply managerial economics principles to healthcare technology markets and develop data-driven business strategy.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: Economic AnalysisHealthcare Industry KnowledgeRevenue Strategy

Managerial Economics — Final Project Report

5. Assemble Your Dream Team – Cloud Computing Final Project

Course: Cloud Computing (IT-8003) | Term: Fall 2, 2024-2025 | Instructor: Dr. Cyndi Lambach

This final project presents the design of an ideal senior management and cross-functional team for executing a cloud adoption initiative focused on Virtual Desktop Infrastructure (VDI). The report maps each department — IT, Business, Finance, Legal, and Administrative — to the four phases of cloud adoption: Assessment, Planning, Implementation, and Optimization. The project demonstrates strategic thinking in cloud technology governance, resource planning, and enterprise-level change management. It reflects the ability to connect technical infrastructure decisions with business strategy and leadership.

6. Business Ethics Simulation – ACME Corporation Performance Reviews

Course: Value Systems and Professional Ethics (BUS-7200) | Term: Spring 1, 2024-2025 | Instructor: Peter Hertzberg

This multi-intersession simulation places students in the role of a career professional at ACME Corporation, navigating ethical and unethical decision points across a simulated career spanning ages 23 to 62. Across eight intersessions, performance review self-assessments reflect on salary goals, promotion outcomes, work-life balance, and the consequences of ethical versus unethical workplace decisions. The simulation reinforces the long-term personal and financial costs of ethical lapses and the value of integrity, transparency, and regulatory compliance. This artifact demonstrates ethical reasoning, reflective practice, and the integration of personal values into professional decision-making.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: Ethical LeadershipProfessional IntegrityReflective Practice

BUS 7200 EMBA — Business Ethics Simulation

7. Etsy's Approach to Knowledge Management

Course: Management Information Systems (BUS-7700) | Term: Spring 2, 2024-2025 | Instructor: Dr. Amy Tidwell

This essay contrasts two organizational models of knowledge management: Canadian Tire's fragmented, analog-dependent franchise network versus Etsy's digitally integrated community intelligence platform. Using frameworks from Nonaka & Takeuchi, Davenport & Beck, and Wenger et al., the paper argues that transitioning to digital Knowledge Management Systems (KMS) is essential for organizational survival in the modern economy. The essay explores how KMS can democratize innovation, reduce cognitive overload, and transform frontline employees into active decision-makers. This artifact demonstrates understanding of information systems strategy, organizational learning, and digital transformation.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: Information Systems StrategyOrganizational LearningDigital Transformation

8. Sony's Battle for Video Game Supremacy: An Organizational Behavior Analysis

Course: Organizational Behavior – Theory (BUS-7000) | Term: Summer 1, 2024-2025 | Instructor: Professor Jackson

This group project examines Sony Interactive Entertainment through the lens of Organizational Behavior theory. The paper identifies three core OB challenges — power struggles between the American and Japanese divisions, interdepartmental conflicts rooted in misaligned incentives, and leadership indecision during innovation cycles. Using frameworks such as French and Raven's Bases of Social Power, Thomas's Conflict Process Theory, Contingency Leadership Theory, and Bolman and Deal's Four-Frame Model, the team developed a SWOT analysis and actionable OB recommendations. This artifact demonstrates mastery in team dynamics, conflict resolution, leadership analysis, and the application of evidence-based management theory.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: Leadership & Team DynamicsCritical ThinkingCollaborative Problem-Solving

9. Implementation of an Outpatient Visit Management System

Course: Database Architecture & Design (IT-8100) | Term: Summer 2, 2024-2025 | Instructor: Dr. Amy Tidwell

This project documents the implementation phase of a relational database system for managing outpatient visits at a healthcare clinic, built on Microsoft Access. The database captures patient information, provider assignments, appointment scheduling, services rendered, and departmental roles. The assignment focuses on DBMS selection rationale, database creation and population, constraint enforcement, and the development of user-facing forms, queries, and reports for clinical staff. This artifact demonstrates proficiency in database design, healthcare data architecture, and translating operational requirements into functional information systems.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: Database ArchitectureHealthcare ITSystems Implementation

10. Capital Structure and Shareholder Value: ETI's Recapitalization Strategy

Course: Managerial Finance (BUS-7600) | Term: Fall 1, 2025-2026 | Instructor: William Hecht

This case study evaluates how Electronic Timing, Inc. (ETI) should allocate a $30 million windfall from a product sale. Four capital allocation alternatives are analyzed — a one-time special dividend, debt reduction with operational expansion, a stock repurchase program, and a regular dividend policy — through the lens of corporate finance theory. Each option's implications for shareholder value, financial flexibility, and long-term growth are assessed, with healthcare industry analogies to illustrate cross-sector applicability. This artifact demonstrates financial modeling skills, shareholder value analysis, and the ability to evaluate competing strategic financial decisions.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: Corporate FinanceShareholder Value AnalysisStrategic Decision-Making

11. Smart Inventory Management System Using IoT and Predictive Analytics

Course: Systems Analytics & Enterprise Management (IT-7000) | Term: Fall 2, 2025-2026 | Instructor: Professor Maria Khater

This comprehensive project designs a Smart Inventory Management System that integrates IoT sensors, machine learning demand forecasting, and Microsoft Azure cloud infrastructure to address the $1.75 trillion global problem of inventory distortion. The system fulfills five core requirements: real-time data collection, predictive analytics, a visual monitoring dashboard, secure remote access, and automated notifications. A five-phase, 10–12 week implementation roadmap is provided, along with projected business outcomes including a 30–40% reduction in inventory carrying costs and 95% inventory accuracy. This artifact showcases skills in enterprise systems design, cloud architecture, and data-driven operations management.

MBA Outcomes Demonstrated: Systems ThinkingEnterprise Technology DesignBusiness Impact Analysis
